Some Facts about the Planet Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System.
It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined,
and slightly less than one one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.
The video on the right shows how Jupiter rotates around itself!

About Jupiter

  • Mass: 1.89813 × 1027 kg
  • Equatorial diameter: 139,820 km
  • Surface Gravity: 24.79 m/s²
  • Average Temperature: -150°C

Some Facts about Jupiter

  1. Jupiter is the fastest spinning planet in the Solar System.
  2. The Great Red Spot has been around for a long time.
  3. Jupiter's magnetic field is 14 times stronger than Earth's.
  4. Jupiter takes 9hr 56m to complete one rotation.
  5. The clouds on Jupiter are only 50 km thick.
